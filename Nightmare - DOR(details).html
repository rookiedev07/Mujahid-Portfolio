Nightmare Dusk of Regret

Concept Arts:

Nightmare: Dusk of Regret

Genre: 2D Story-Driven Parkour Survival

Target Audience: Story-Driven Game Enthusiasts

Time to Complete Design: 2 days taken

Core Concept:

Nightmare: Dusk of Regret is a 2D side-scrolling survival game that blends emotional storytelling with intense parkour gameplay. Players follow Maxwell, a brilliant scientist whose experiment to advance medical science causes a catastrophic zombie outbreak. His research, intended to improve human health, turns his entire team, including his best friend James, into infected creatures.

Set in a dystopian world ravaged by the virus, Maxwell, consumed by guilt, isolates himself to create an antidote that could redeem his past. As he navigates the decaying urban environment, Maxwell battles the infected and confronts painful memories, determined to save humanity and redeem himself.

Prologue – The Last Hope: Maxwell, driven by guilt, begins his journey to find the ingredients for the antidote. He battles through dangerous wastelands, encountering the Infected and remnants of the past.

Act 1 – The Search for the Cure: Maxwell uncovers clues about the antidote’s ingredients while facing emotional and physical challenges. Flashbacks to his time with James deepen his resolve.

Act 2 – The Struggle: As Maxwell gets closer to completing the antidote, he faces tougher enemies and environmental hazards. He realizes that his quest for a cure is also about redemption, requiring a personal sacrifice.

Act 3 – The Final Push: Maxwell synthesizes the antidote but succumbs to the toxins in his body. As he delivers the cure, the world begins to heal, but he dies, his final thoughts with James.

Epilogue – Hope at a great cost: Maxwell’s hard-work restores hope, but Maxwell Dies due to overexposure to a toxin used for creating the antidote. The world begins to rebuild, unaware of the man who gave everything to save them.


Key Features:

1. Parkour Movement: Maxwell uses parkour to navigate a decaying urban landscape, improving his abilities over time to dodge zombies and reach difficult areas. The gameplay combines survival, parkour action, and emotional storytelling, with Maxwell's inner turmoil impacting his survival.

2. Combat with Zombies: Combat is tense, with limited resources. Maxwell uses improvised weapons and bare hands for survival, with quick-time events and timed attacks adding intensity. Avoiding combat is essential to conserve resources.

3. Emotional Narrative and Flashbacks: Players uncover Maxwell's emotional journey through flashbacks and cutscenes, revealing his guilt and regret. The non-linear narrative is shaped by player choices, affecting how the world responds to Maxwell.
